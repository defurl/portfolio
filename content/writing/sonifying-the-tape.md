## Sonifying the Tape

A price chart is a compression. It throws away everything about *how* a market
moved and keeps only where it ended up. The tape — the raw sequence of trades
and quotes — has a texture the chart cannot show: the stutter before a print,
the silence of a thin book, the sudden density when volume arrives.

I started mapping that texture to sound because I wanted to feel a market
without watching it. Volatility to the tempo of a bassline. Volume to the
density of soft percussive ticks. Index direction to the key the whole thing
sits in — major when the day is green, a flattened third when it is not.

The surprising part was not that it worked. It was that after a week of
leaving it running, I could tell what kind of day it was from the next room.
The ear picks up rhythm changes the eye glosses over.

This site's voxel city runs on the same idea: the buildings are the chart,
but the soundscape is the tape. One is for looking. The other is for knowing
without looking.
